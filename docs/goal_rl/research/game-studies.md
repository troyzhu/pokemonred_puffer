# Research notes — game studies (G-series)

Compact notes on game-studies and HCI literature grounding the
goal_rl design. Source IDs map to [sources.md](sources.md).

## G01 — SDT in Games

- Ryan/Rigby/Przybylski 2006: applied SDT to video games — games
  satisfy competence, autonomy, and (in multiplayer) relatedness
  needs; this predicts engagement. [[S03](sources.md#s03)]
- Przybylski/Rigby/Ryan 2010: integrative motivational model of game
  engagement built on SDT. [[S04](sources.md#s04)]
- **Design role.** Directly grounds Layer 1 of
  [C01](../concepts/C01-three-layer-value-schema.md).

## G02 — GameFlow

- Sweetser & Wyeth 2005: eight elements — concentration, challenge,
  skills, control, clear goals, feedback, immersion, social
  interaction. [[S17](sources.md#s17)]
- Variety identified as a sustaining flow condition.
  [[S17](sources.md#s17)]
- **Design role.** Grounds `diversity` as a Layer-2 goal; supports
  the anti-repetition signal in the "engagement" evaluator view.

## G03 — Gamer Motivation Model

- Yee 2006: factor analysis on online-game players → achievement,
  social, immersion factors. [[S18](sources.md#s18)]
- Evidence-based refinement of Bartle's player types.
  [[S18](sources.md#s18)]
- **Design role.** Shows that mastery-as-distinct-from-progress is
  empirically supported (two separate Layer-2 categories in
  [C01](../concepts/C01-three-layer-value-schema.md)).
